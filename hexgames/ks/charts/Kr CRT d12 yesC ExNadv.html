DX – all defending units destroyed

AX – all attacking units destroyed

Ex – all defending units lost, and an equal number of attacking (unmodified) combat factors lost

D4 – defender loses 4 combat factors or retreats 4 hexes (all units), or any combination adding up to 4 total

D2 - defender loses 2 combat factors or retreats 2 hexes (all units), or any combination adding up to 2 total

AL4/AL6 – attacker loses 4 or 6 combat factors

A4 – attacker loses up to 4 combat factors (0 to 4) – for each lost all units may advance that many hexes

Adv4 – all attacking units may advance 4 hexes

Adv2 – all attacking units advance 2 hexes


  • results on defenders are applied before results on attackers

  • attacker decides the retreat route of defending units – but must choose a legal retreat if it exists – if a defenders cannot retreat a minimum of half (rounded up) the expected hexes then it is eliminated

  • attacker chooses all advance routes – can advance less than the amount available or not advances


Attacker, at his option may reduce odds by 1 column for each of the following:

  • get an automatic ad2

  • to upgrade an automatic ad2 to automatic ad4

  • to get an automatic ‘defender retreat 2’

  • to upgrade to ‘automatic retreat 4’


attacker can combine the automatic advances to any advance rolled on table – but can never achieve more than ‘advance 4’.


defender who has an ‘automatic’ retreat against them cannot offset those retreats against a D2 or D4, but must take combat loses (but, if have automatic retreat 2 and rolls D4: may take 2 combat loses and retreat-2 from D4 and so has 2 loses and retreat-4 in total). Defender may not have more than ‘retreat 4’ as a final result


example: 7-1 may become 3-1 with automatic ad4 and automatic retreat 4, 1-1 may become 1-3 with retreat 4


Attacker may not reduce odds in this way to less than 1-3 (eg minimum of 3-1 odds needed to get automatic ‘adv4’ and ‘retreat-4’)


NOTES:

  • this table is a direct 1 to 1 ‘translation’ of the original Kriegspiel Matrix, but some choice has been built into the CRT as there is in the Matrix CRT. For example D4 – defender can choose to retreat 4 as if he has chosen ‘Abandon Position’ or he can lose factors and not retreat as if he had chosen a no-retreat option. Similarly A4 – attacker can take casualties and advance as if he’d chosen ‘Escalating Assault’ or ‘Blitzkrieg’ or he can take no casualties as if he had gone for ‘Engage & Hold’ but then gets no advance

  • At 2-1 there is never any advantage, or reason, for the Defender to choose ‘Hold-at-all-Costs’, so ‘Standfast’ is used twice instead by the defender (the difference is that a die-roll of 12 becomes “A4” rather than “DX/A4”).

  • All the DX/AX results (mutual destruction) have been turned into Exchange results, this slightly favours the attacker. In many cases the attacker gets an advance if he has any units left. You might want to get rid of attacker advances after Ex, as in the original matrix all the attackers would have been eliminated and so none of them would have been able to advance

  • Rolling 12 at 3-1 should be DX/AL6Adv4, but I have changed this to Ex/Adv4 as an alternative, as it seems to fit in better with the rest of the table – change back if you like
