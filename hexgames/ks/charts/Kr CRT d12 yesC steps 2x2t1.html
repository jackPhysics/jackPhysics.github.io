DX – all defending units lose 1 step

AX – all attacking units lose 1 step

D2 – defender loses 2 steps total or retreats all units 4 hexes, or any combination adding up to 4 in total where each 1 step = 2 hexes eg 2 steps total and no retreat; 1 step total and all retreat 2 hexes; all retreat 4 hexes

D1 - defender loses 1 step or retreats all units 2 hexes

AL2/AL3 – attacker loses 2 or 3 steps

A2 – attacker loses up to 2 steps (0, 1 or 2) – for each step lost all units may advance double that many hexes eg lose 0 steps = no advance; lose 1 step = advance-2; lose 2 steps = advance-4

Adv4 – any surviving attacking units may advance 4 hexes

Adv2 – any surviving attacking units advance 2 hexes


  • results on defenders are applied before results on attackers

  • attacker decides the retreat route of defending units – but must choose a legal retreat if it exists – if a defender unit cannot retreat a minimum of half (rounded up) the expected hexes then it is eliminated

  • attacker chooses all advance routes – can advance less than the amount available or not advances


Attacker, at his option may reduce odds by 1 column for each of the following ‘modifiers’:

  • get an automatic Adv2

  • to upgrade an automatic Adv2 to automatic Adv4

  • to get an automatic DR2

  • to upgrade automatic DR2 to automatic DR4


Attacker can combine the automatic advances to any advance rolled on table – but can never achieve more than ‘advance 4’.


defender who has an ‘automatic’ retreat against them cannot offset those retreats against a D1 or D2, but must take combat loses (but, if have automatic retreat 2 and rolls D2: may take 1 step loss and retreat-2 from D4 and so has 1 step loss and retreat-4 in total). Defender may not have more than ‘retreat 4’ as a final result


example: 7-1 may become 3-1 with automatic ad4 and automatic retreat 4, 1-1 may become 1-3 with retreat 4


Attacker may not reduce odds in this way to less than 1-3 (eg minimum of 1-1 odds needed to get automatic ‘Adv4’ or ‘DR4’; or minimum of 3-1 odds needed to get automatic ‘Adv4’ and ‘DR4’)


NOTES:

  • this table is a direct 1 to 1 ‘translation’ of the original Kriegspiel Matrix, but some choice has been built into the CRT as there is in the Matrix CRT. For example D4 – defender can choose to retreat 4 as if he has chosen ‘Abandon Position’ or he can lose factors and not retreat as if he had chosen a no-retreat option. Similarly A4 – attacker can take casualties and advance as if he’d chosen ‘Escalating Assault’ or ‘Blitzkrieg’ or he can take no casualties and no advance as if he had gone for ‘Engage & Hold’

  • There are two possible 2-1 Columns, here’s why: In the Matrix CRT, at 2-1, it is possible for the attacker to get an A-Elim (‘Escalating Assault’ vs ‘Fighting Withdrawal’ = A-Elim D-Elim). Now this could be an attack against a doubled defence position at odds of 2.9 to 1, meaning there are almost 6 times as many attacking combat factors lost as defence combat factors – if the defender was an 8 factor unit, the attacker might be losing as much as 47 factors! That’s almost half of either Army! This is a case where, when using the Matrix CRT, the attacker would never choose ‘Escalating Assault’. And on the defensive side there is never any advantage, at 2-1, for the Defender to choose ‘Hold-at-all-Costs’.

  • 2-1A is where the attacker is willing to risk A-Elim, and so ‘Escalating Assault’ is a possible choice and 2-1B is where the attacker does not want to risk A-Elim, and so ignores ‘Escalating Assault’. In both options the Defender does not consider ‘Hold-at-all-Costs’ as a choice, and so those outcomes are always ignored (the only difference, because of this choice by the Defender, for 2-1A, is that a roll of 12 is now just A4 rather than DX/A4).
