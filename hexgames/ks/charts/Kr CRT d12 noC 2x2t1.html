DX/AX – all defending or attacking units destroyed

DL4/AL4/AL6 – defender or attacker loses 4 (or 6) combat factors

DR4/DR2 – any surviving defending units must retreat 4 or 2 hexes (if cannot retreat at least 2 hexes then unit eliminated)

Ad4/Ad2 – any surviving attacking units may advance up to 4 or 2 hexes


  • results on defenders are applied before results on attackers

  • attacker decides the retreat route of defending units – but must choose a legal retreat if it exists – if a defenders cannot retreat a minimum of 2 hexes then it is eliminated

  • attacker chooses all advance routes – can advance less than the amount available or not advance at all


Attacker, at his option may reduce odds by 1 column for each of the following ‘modifiers’:

  • get an automatic Ad2

  • to upgrade an automatic Ad2 to automatic Ad4

  • to get an automatic DR2

  • to upgrade automatic DR2 to automatic DR4


Attacker can combine the automatic advances to any advance rolled on table – but can never achieve more than ‘advance 4’.


Defender must add ‘automatic’ retreat against any retreats they roll. Defender may not have more than ‘retreat 4’ as a final result


examples: 7-1 may become 3-1 with automatic Ad4 and automatic DR4, 1-1 may become 1-3 with DR4


Attacker may not reduce odds in this way to less than 1-3 (eg minimum of 1-1 odds needed to get automatic ‘Ad4’ or ‘DR4’; or minimum of 3-1 odds needed to get automatic ‘Ad4’ and ‘DR4’)


Any attack at less than 1-3 counts as 1-3.


Any attack greater than 3-1 rolls on the 3-1 column – but extra odds can be used to ‘buy’ modifiers


NOTES:

  • this table is a direct 1 to 1 ‘translation’ of the original Kriegspiel Matrix, an alternative table exists which has some measure of choice, similar to the choices available using the Matrix CRT.

  • There are two possible 2-1 Columns, here’s why: In the Matrix CRT, at 2-1, it is possible for the attacker to get an A-Elim (‘Escalating Assault’ vs ‘Fighting Withdrawal’ = A-Elim D-Elim). Now this could be an attack against a doubled defence position at odds of 2.9 to 1, meaning there are almost 6 times as many attacking combat factors lost as defence combat factors – if the defender was an 8 factor unit, the attacker might be losing as much as 47 factors! That’s almost half of either Army! This is a case where, when using the Matrix CRT, the attacker would never choose ‘Escalating Assault’. And on the defensive side there is never any advantage, at 2-1, for the Defender to choose ‘Hold-at-all-Costs’.

  • 2-1A is where the all choices are possible and 2-1B is where the attacker does not want to risk A-Elim, so ignores ‘Escalating Assault’ and the Defender does not consider ‘Hold-at-all-Costs’ as a choice.

  • If you want to play using a CRT table that exactly matches the Matrix CRT then ignore 2-1B and only use 2-1A as the 2-1 column
