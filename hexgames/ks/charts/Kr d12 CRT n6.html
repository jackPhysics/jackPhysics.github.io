DX – all defending units destroyed

AX – all attacking units destroyed

Ex – side with smaller total combat factors (unmodified) loses all units, and other side must lose an equal number of (unmodified) combat factors

D6/D4 – defender loses 6/4 combat factors or retreats 4 hexes (all units), or any combination adding up to 6 or 4 total

D2 - defender loses 2 combat factors or retreats 2 hexes (all units), or any combination adding up to 2 total

DR4/DR2 - defender must retreat 4 or 2 hexes – if cannot retreat at least half (rounded down) then unit is eliminated

AL2/AL4 – attacker loses 2 or 4 combat factors

A4 – attacker loses up to 4 combat factors (0 to 4) – for each factor lost all units may advance that many hexes

Adv4 – any surviving attacking units may advance 4 hexes

Adv2 – any surviving attacking units advance 2 hexes

A2/Adv2 – attacker advances 2 hexes, and can lose 1 or 2 factors to advance an extra 1 or 2 hexes – all units involved in attack get to advance, only 1 unit needs to lose factors


  • results on defenders are applied before results on attackers

  • attacker decides the retreat route of defending units – but must choose a legal retreat if it exists – if a defender unit cannot retreat a minimum of half (rounded up) the expected hexes then it is eliminated

  • attacker chooses all advance routes – can advance less than the amount available or not advances


NOTES:

  • this table is a direct 1 to 1 ‘translation’ of the original Kriegspiel Matrix, but some choice has been built into the CRT as there is in the Matrix CRT. For example D4 – defender can choose to retreat 4 as if he has chosen ‘Abandon Position’ or he can lose factors and not retreat as if he had chosen a no-retreat option. Similarly A4 – attacker can take casualties and advance as if he’d chosen ‘Escalating Assault’ or ‘Blitzkrieg’ or he can take no casualties and not advance as if he had gone for ‘Engage & Hold’

  • the original table causes large casualties to the attacker with the many DX/AX results, particularly when the defender is in a doubled defensive position. So here all DX/AX have been changed to Ex results (where I play that you only have to match the unmodified factors of the smaller side – but even if you play matching doubled defence factors the attacker will still usually lose less than straight DX/AX).

  • In the original table because the attacker loses all his units then obviously there is no advance after combat. But if the result is now an exchange then some attacking units might survive the exchange and so possible advances (as dictated by the matrix) are added into the results.

  • If the larger side cannot break down their combat factors to exactly match the smaller side’s then there are different options:

    • original rules: larger side must lose at least or MORE. eg attacker has 8 against defender with 6. Attacker must lose all 8 factors

    • or, add the extra factors lost to that side’s ‘replacement points’ eg in the above example the attacker loses all 8 combat factors but gains 2 ‘replacement points’

    • or, mark the unit with a chit to show its new reduced strength eg in the above example the 8 factor unit would have ‘2’ chit placed on it – there’s an old SPI game that uses this system but I cannot remember which one it is – you’ll find that it’s only used quite rarely, at most only 3 units at a time will be reduced. The units can have their strength built up again using ‘replacement points’

  • The columns below 1-3 and above 3-1 are calculated off the 1-3 and 3-1 column but with extra advances and retreats taken away or added – for a fuller explanation see the optional rules discussed in earlier versions of these CRTs (numbers 2 to 5)
