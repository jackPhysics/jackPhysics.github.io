HERACLEA (280 BC)

 

Pyrrhus: 1OMS, 5PS, 12PP, SMC, 1HC, 4BW, 1SK, 4El, 1”l”, 1”2”. VP 111, PL 67. Deploy: F-3. Stacking: 4. Leadership: Mt. Ex.

 

Roman.: 4MS, 681, 1OSD, 6SK, 4LC, 1”2”. VP 68, PL 51. Deploy: M-3.

Stacking: 3.

 

Game Length: 12 Game-Turns.

 

Victory Conditions: Pyrrhus must capture the castra (village at M-3). Romans must achieve a Marginal Victory. If both are fulfilled, the Romans win.

 

Terrain Modifications: none

 

Historical Note: As the Romans expanded their territory in central Italy, they came into contact with the Greek city-states in the south. The rowdy Romans couldn’t seem to get along with anybody, and the Greeks were no exception. The Greeks soon felt compelled to ask for protection from the mainland, and shortly King Pyrrhus of Epirus landed with 25,000 men. This kept the Romans at bay until they could amass a superior force, which they did the next year. The 35,000 Romans, not content with a simple show of farce prior to the battle, continued to advance across the river Sins, directly behind which were the Greek positions. Pyrrhus attacked and, after hard fighting, routed the Romans with his cavalry, taking 2000 captives. About 7000 Romans and 4000 Greeks were lost.
